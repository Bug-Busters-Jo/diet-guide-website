Body mass index (BMI)

Body mass index (BMI) is a common tool for deciding whether a person has an appropriate body weight. It measures a personâ€™s weight in relation to their height.

According to the National Institutes of Health (NIH):

  • A BMI of less than 18.5 means that a person is underweight.
  • A BMI of between 18.5 and 24.9 is ideal.
  • A BMI of between 25 and 29.9 is overweight.
  • A BMI over 30 indicates obesity.
